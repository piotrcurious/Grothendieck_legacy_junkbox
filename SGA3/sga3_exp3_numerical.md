Viewed through numerical methods, this exposé is a theory of exact first-order lifting. The setup is: start with data over a reduced base , thicken it by a nilpotent ideal , and then lift the data step by step through the thickenings ; at each step one records obstructions and classifies the lifts that exist. The author explicitly says the problem is to lift data from  to  “de proche en proche,” with obstructions and classification at each stage. 

In numerical-methods language, this is very close to a continuation / perturbation / linearization framework, except the perturbation is nilpotent, so the linearization is exact rather than approximate. Section 0 builds the analogue of the Jacobian machinery: it defines the functor , the tangent-like object , and expresses maps into  using relative differentials  paired with the nilpotent ideal . That is the paper’s coordinate-free version of “value + infinitesimal correction.” 

The logical core is obstruction theory. In section 1, the paper recasts extension problems in cohomological terms: extensions of group objects with a section are controlled by , the ambiguity of sections by , and associativity of an admissible composition law by a class in . Numerically, you can think of these as the residual equations that decide whether a correction step can be completed, and if it can, how many degrees of freedom remain. 

Then the rest of the document applies that template to three concrete lifting problems named in the introduction: extension of group morphisms (section 2), extension of group structures (section 3), and extension of closed subgroups (section 4). The pattern is the same each time: identify the linearized coefficient object, compute the relevant cohomology or Ext group, and read off existence and uniqueness up to equivalence. For closed subgroups, section 4 even starts with a general exact-sequence lemma in an abelian category, then turns it into a subgroup-extension criterion; later results give conditions under which the obstruction vanishes and the solution space becomes a torsor under a Hom-group. 

So, in one sentence: this PDF is an exact algebraic-geometric analogue of a multilevel linearized solver, where the “iterate,” “correction,” and “failure mode” are encoded by nilpotent thickenings, tangent modules, and cohomology classes.
