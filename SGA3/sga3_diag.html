what changes, what does not

The number can keep growing forever, but the point on the circle repeats every 360°. In the page, the raw angle stores the growing number, while the wrapped angle stores the position on one turn of the circle.

the SGA3 distinction

A diagonalizable group is already in the standard form D(M). A group of multiplicative type may not be in that form on the base, but after a suitable faithfully flat quasi-compact cover it becomes diagonalizable. That is the whole point of the comparison.

terms on screen

The standard diagonalizable form built from an ordinary commutative group M.

A group that is locally the same after a cover, but not visibly standard on the base.

not diagonalizable
